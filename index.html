Be prepared for the open road (or sky) with our top three travel tips for pets!

Current ID

Even with current ID tags, make sure your pets are microchipped and your contact info is up to date, which will increase their chances of being returned safely.

Do your homework

If you’re traveling by plane or train, every carrier has different rules and regulations, so it’s best to talk to a live person to verify the safest way to bring your pet.

Bring everything they need

Pets may find traveling stressful, so bring their familiar food, medication, treats, carriers, leashes, bedding, and toys to make them feel more safe and comfortable.
